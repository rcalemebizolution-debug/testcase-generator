BUSINESS REQUIREMENTS DOCUMENT
Customer Registration

Document ID: BRD-REG
Version: 1.0
Status: Draft for QA testing
Owner: Product Management

1. PURPOSE
This document defines the business requirements for customer self-registration in the Casecraft sample customer portal. It is intended to test requirement extraction, approval, traceability, and Development test-case generation.

2. BUSINESS OBJECTIVE
Allow a new customer to create a secure account using an email address, verify ownership of that address, and access the portal after successful activation.

3. SCOPE
In scope:
- Customer registration form
- Client-side and server-side validation
- Email uniqueness validation
- Password policy enforcement
- Terms and privacy consent
- Email verification
- Registration audit events
- Basic abuse prevention

Out of scope:
- Social login
- Enterprise single sign-on
- Administrator-created accounts
- Paid subscription selection
- Identity document verification

4. ACTORS
- Visitor: an unauthenticated person creating an account
- Customer: a registered and verified portal user
- Support agent: an authorized employee who can view registration status but cannot see passwords

5. FUNCTIONAL REQUIREMENTS

BRD-REG-001: The system must allow an unauthenticated visitor to open the customer registration page.

BRD-REG-002: The registration form must require the visitor's first name, last name, email address, password, password confirmation, acceptance of the Terms of Service, and acceptance of the Privacy Policy.

BRD-REG-003: The system must trim leading and trailing spaces from first name, last name, and email address before validating or saving the registration.

BRD-REG-004: The system must treat email addresses as case-insensitive when checking uniqueness.

BRD-REG-005: The system must reject registration when the submitted email address is already associated with an existing account.

BRD-REG-006: The system must validate that the email address follows a valid email format before submitting the registration.

BRD-REG-007: The password must contain at least 8 characters, including at least one uppercase letter, one lowercase letter, one number, and one special character.

BRD-REG-008: The system must reject registration when the password and password-confirmation values do not match.

BRD-REG-009: The visitor must accept both the Terms of Service and Privacy Policy before registration can be submitted.

BRD-REG-010: After valid registration data is submitted, the system must create the account in a Pending Verification status.

BRD-REG-011: The system must send a verification email to the registered email address after creating a Pending Verification account.

BRD-REG-012: The email-verification link must expire 24 hours after it is issued.

BRD-REG-013: When a valid, unexpired verification link is used, the system must change the account status from Pending Verification to Active.

BRD-REG-014: The system must reject an expired verification link and offer the customer an option to request a new verification email.

BRD-REG-015: The system must not allow a Pending Verification account to log in until email verification is completed.

BRD-REG-016: The system must display a confirmation message after successful registration without revealing whether any other email address is registered.

BRD-REG-017: If the registration service is temporarily unavailable, the system must display a recoverable error and must not create a partial customer account.

BRD-REG-018: The system must prevent duplicate accounts when the visitor submits the registration form multiple times during the same request.

BRD-REG-019: The system must limit registration attempts from the same source to 5 attempts within 15 minutes and display a retry message when the limit is reached.

BRD-REG-020: The system must record an audit event for account creation, verification-email issuance, successful verification, failed verification, and rate-limit enforcement.

6. DATA AND PRIVACY REQUIREMENTS

BRD-REG-021: Passwords must never be stored or logged in plain text.

BRD-REG-022: Registration error messages and application logs must not expose the submitted password or password confirmation.

BRD-REG-023: Support agents must be able to view the account's registration and verification status but must not be able to view password data.

BRD-REG-024: The system must store the date, time, and policy version associated with the customer's Terms of Service and Privacy Policy consent.

7. ACCESSIBILITY AND USABILITY REQUIREMENTS

BRD-REG-025: Every registration input must have a visible label and an accessible programmatic name.

BRD-REG-026: Validation errors must identify the affected field and must be available to keyboard and screen-reader users.

BRD-REG-027: The registration flow must be operable using only a keyboard.

BRD-REG-028: The registration page must preserve valid non-sensitive entries when a validation error is displayed, but it must not preserve password fields after a full page reload.

8. ACCEPTANCE SUMMARY
The Registration feature is accepted when a new visitor can submit valid details, receive and use a verification email, activate the account, and log in; invalid, duplicate, insecure, expired, inaccessible, and excessive-attempt scenarios must be handled according to the requirements above.

9. ASSUMPTIONS
- The email delivery provider is available and configured.
- Terms of Service and Privacy Policy versions are maintained by the product team.
- Rate-limit thresholds can be configured by an administrator in a future release.

10. OPEN QUESTIONS
- Should unverified accounts be deleted automatically after a retention period?
- Should the customer be allowed to change the email address while verification is pending?
- Is CAPTCHA required after repeated failed registration attempts?
